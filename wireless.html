WIRELESS

Wireless networking connects devices without cables using radio waves. It allows flexible movement and easy access but can be affected by distance and interference. It's commonly used in homes, schools, and public spaces.

Hardware Needed

  • Wireless Network Interface Card: Lets devices connect to a wireless network
  • Wireless Router: Sends out the Wi-Fi signal and connects to the internet
  • Modem: Connects your network to your Internet Service Provider (ISP)
  • Access Point: Extends the Wi-Fi signal in larger areas.
  • Antenna (built-in or external): Boosts wireless signal strength.

Wireless: Pros

  • Easy to set up and expand
  • Devices can move freely without losing connection
  • No messy cables needed
  • Good for public and large spaces
  • Can connect many devices at once

Wireless: Cons

  • Slower and less stable than wired connections
  • Signal can be blocked by walls or other objects
  • More vulnerable to hacking and interference
  • Connection quality may drop with distance
  • Can be affected by other wireless devices nearby
