wired

Wired networking is a method of connecting devices using physical cables, usually Ethernet. It provides a reliable and stable connection with faster speeds and lower latency compared to wireless networks. Wired connections are commonly used in homes, offices, and data centers where performance and security are important.

Hardware Needed

  • Network Interface Card (NIC) – Allows devices to connect to a wired network
  • Ethernet Cables – Physical cables used to connect devices
  • Switch or Hub – Distributes the network signal to multiple devices
  • Router – Directs data between networks and often connects to the internet
  • Modem – Connects your local network to your Internet Service Provider (ISP)
  • Patch Panel (optional) – Organizes and manages cable connections in larger networks
  • RJ-45 Connectors – Standard connectors used on Ethernet cables
